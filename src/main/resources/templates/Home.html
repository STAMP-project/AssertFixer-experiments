The Institute Archives and Special Collections (IASC) at MIT Libraries collects, preserves, and makes available digital material as well as paper and other analog formats.

This Digital Transfer Tool provides the ability to transfer digital collections from Institute offices and from donors of personal papers to the IASC.

What does the Institute Archives and Special Collections (IASC) collect?

The IASC is interested in:

  • records that are generated or received by the various administrative and academic offices of the Institute in the conduct of their business,
  • teaching and research materials
  • correspondence with colleagues or professional organizations,
  • committee files
  • financial records (summary level, budgets) and annual reports
  • and more...

Please note - the IASC only collects inactive material. If you are actively using the records, then they are not ready to be donated to the Archives.

For more information on preparing and submitting materials, visit the FAQ section.
